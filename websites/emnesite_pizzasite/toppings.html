Pecorino

Pecorino Romano is a hard, salty Italian cheese, often used for grating, made with sheep's milk. Pecorino Romano means directly translated "sheep's [cheese] of Rome".

Pecorino is often used on pasta dishes, its distinctive aromatic and pleasantly sharp, very salty flavour led to it being preferred for some Italian pasta dishes with highly flavoured sauces, especially those of Roman origin, such as bucatini all'amatriciana, spaghetti alla carbonara, pasta alla gricia, and spaghetti cacio e pepe (which it is a main ingredient in this dish). The sharpness depends on the period of maturation, which varies from five months for a table cheese to eight months or longer for a grating cheese. Most pecorino cheeses are classified as grana and are granular, hard and sharply flavored.

Mozzarella

Buffalo mozzarella, is a mozzarella made from the milk of Italian Mediterranean buffalo. It is a dairy product traditionally manufactured in Campania, especially in the provinces of Caserta and Salerno. Buffalo mozzarella is eaten with calzone, vegetable, salad, on pizza (a low moisture content buffalo mozzarella is preferred), on grilled bread, with tomatoes, or by itself accompanied by olive oil.

San Marzano

San Marzano tomatoes are thin and more pointy. The flesh is much thicker with fewer seeds, and the taste is stronger, sweeter, and less acidic. San Marzano tomatoes have been designated as the only tomatoes that can be used for Vera Pizza Napoletana (True Neapolitan Pizza). Then you know what to get when you are throwing a pizza party.

Pepperoni or Salami

Pepperoni is an American variety of spicy salami made from cured pork and beef seasoned with paprika or other chili pepper. Prior to cooking, pepperoni is characteristically soft, slightly smoky, and bright red.[1] Thinly sliced pepperoni is one of the most popular pizza toppings in American pizzerias. Delicious!

Pepperoni, an Italian-American creation, is a cured dry sausage, with similarities to the spicy salamis of southern Italy on which it is based, such as salsiccia or soppressata. Pepperoni is usually softer in texture, and is usually produced with the use of an artificial casing.

Prosciutto/ Ham

Prosciutto or Prosciutto crudo, is Italian uncooked, unsmoked, and dry-cured ham. Prosciutto crudo is usually served thinly sliced. Sliced prosciutto crudo in Italy is often served as an antipasto(starters), wrapped around grissini, or accompanied with melon or figs. It is also eaten as accompaniment to cooked spring vegetables, such as asparagus or peas. It may be included in a simple pasta sauce made with cream, or a Tuscan dish of tagliatelle and vegetables. It is used in stuffings for other meats, such as veal, as a wrap around veal or steak, in a filled bread, and as a pizza topping.

Fun fact: pineapple on pizza - Hawaiian pizza is a pizza originating in Canada, and is traditionally topped with pineapple, tomato sauce, cheese, and either ham or bacon. at first it was not popular but became localy popular and later global. The name of this creation is, in fact, actually not directly inspired by the U.S. state of Hawaii at all; it is said that the creator chose the name Hawaiian after the brand of canned pineapple he was using at the time.
